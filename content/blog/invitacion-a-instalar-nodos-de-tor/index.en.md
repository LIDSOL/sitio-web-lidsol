Over the past 4 years, TELMEX blocked access to the *directory authorities* of the Tor network[^1][^2], which prevented the creation of Tor nodes within TELMEX’s network.

TELMEX did not lift this blockade by magic, but thanks to the efforts of many people — [Gunnar Wolf tells that story better](https://gwolf.org/2020/06/tor-from-telmex-when-i-say-achievement-unlocked-i-mean-it.html).

Now that TELMEX has unblocked the creation of new nodes on its network, all that remains is for anyone interested in privacy to install their own node.

# Why install a Tor node?

Tor is a network that allows anonymous browsing, helping to prevent tracking and censorship on the internet. It is of vital importance for activists, hacktivists, journalists, vulnerable groups, and people living under totalitarian regimes.

The technologies that underpin the Tor network can only guarantee anonymity if there is a large number of nodes distributed evenly around the globe. Currently, the presence of nodes outside European countries or the United States is limited. Installing nodes in Mexico would significantly improve the overall health of the network.

# Who should install a Tor node?

Everyone should install a Tor node! Doing so is simple, and middle relays do not require much maintenance. However, some groups may have a special interest in running one.

## News agencies

Journalism is probably one of the most dangerous professions in Mexico. Tor is one of the most powerful tools available to journalists, as it allows them to exchange documents with sources, conduct investigations, and publish content anonymously — thus avoiding surveillance and censorship attempts by the State, criminal groups, corporations, or any actor threatened by journalistic work. Contributing bandwidth to the Tor network is especially beneficial for news agencies, independent newspapers, and journalists, as it helps ensure that Tor remains available for them and others worldwide.

## Activist groups

Mexico has many activists working on environmental protection, searching for disappeared persons, diversity, feminism, human rights protection, and more. The Tor network provides them with tools to organize freely and anonymously, build movements, and avoid harassment and surveillance by the State or other actors. The health of the Tor network should matter to them, and running a Tor node may be within their reach.

## Bloggers, scientists

Anyone conducting research or writing about sensitive topics can benefit from Tor — for example, individuals publishing controversial or censored opinions in their country, scientists researching censored topics, researchers studying the Tor network itself, or digital security professionals conducting penetration tests for companies.

## Hackers, makers, and students

People who enjoy building new things or embrace the *do-it-yourself* culture — those who want to run their own home “cloud” instead of relying on Google, create their own streaming service for family and friends, or host a server or blog on their personal computer to share with their communities — can benefit from the Tor network and from running their own node to host hidden services within Tor.

# Where to learn how to install a node?

Tor provides excellent information on how to install a node, covering both legal considerations and technical aspects. The right place to find these resources is on its [community site](https://community.torproject.org/relay). There is also a [wiki](https://trac.torproject.org/projects/tor), but it is important to check the last update date before following a guide there, as some articles may be outdated.

Once you have installed your Tor node, remember to join the [relay operators mailing list](https://lists.torproject.org/cgi-bin/mailman/listinfo/tor-relays) to stay informed about security best practices and to ask for or offer help to other volunteers.

Unfortunately, most of the resources above are in English. At LIDSoL, we will be publishing guides in the coming days on how to install nodes and create hidden services on the Tor network. If you would like to install a node and have questions, you can contact the Laboratory through our [Telegram group](https://telegram.me/lidsol).
